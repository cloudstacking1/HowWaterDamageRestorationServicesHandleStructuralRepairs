FAQ's

Water damage restoration is the process of cleaning up and repairing damage caused by water intrusion. This could involve water removal drying, dehumidification, cleaning, sanitizing, and restoring structural elements like drywall, flooring, and furniture. Prompt restoration is essential because water can cause lasting damage to your property and increase the risk of mold growth, which can pose serious health risks.

Water damage can be subtle, but there are several signs that indicate you may need professional restoration services: Visible water: Puddles or standing water in basements, bathrooms, or under sinks. Water stains: Discoloration on ceilings, walls, or floors, often indicating a leak or flooding. Musty odors: A damp, unpleasant smell is a sign of moisture buildup and potential mold growth. Swelling or warping: Wood floors or furniture that swell, buckle, or become deformed due to water exposure.

The timeline for water damage restoration depends on the severity of the damage. Small leaks or minor flooding may only take a few hours to a day to address, while major flooding or long-term water exposure could take several days or even weeks.
